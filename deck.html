Why it wins — and why we keep building

The Focus Dividend.

Every routine thing your agent handles is an interruption you never got — call it ~6 hours a week, per person.
You get the hours back — the system tallies what your agent absorbed, and you cash it in as protected, no-meeting focus time the org honors.
Governed & compounding — approval gates and PII walls keep it safe; every new agent makes every other one faster.
The holdout is the slow node — as agents spread, being out means everything routes through you, by hand. Adoption becomes the easy path.
Per-person flavor → org-wide culture. This is how agents actually get embraced.

Roadmap · the hard parts we're building

What's next.

Least-privilege by default — the agent reads only what's scoped to it and shares only what's explicitly tagged. Never "read all my email," never leak sensitive data.
Lifecycle & offboarding — when someone leaves, their agent is revoked, in-flight work is handed off, and they're removed from the directory — cleanly.
Trust & audit — signed agent-to-agent messages (no impersonation) and a full audit trail — SOC 2-ready.
Cost & scale — per-agent budgets, and multi-tenant connectors so every box carries only its own credentials.
We're not hiding the hard parts. We're sequencing them.
